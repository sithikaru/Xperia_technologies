Product Description

Sony has a habit of making phones no one expects, and no one has thought to ask for. That's how the Xperia Pro came to be. The Xperia 1 II's ultra-expensive alter ego was meant to be used as an external monitor for Sony Alpha cameras... and it also happened to be a phone with 5G connectivity. The Xperia Pro-I, on the other hand, is not an accessory, it's the main act - the phone is the camera. Inside the Pro-I, Sony fitted a 20MP 1.0" Type sensor, and it's the first one this big with phase-detect autofocus on a phone, they point out to get that Sharp out of the conversation. What they don't explicitly mention is that the Pro-I isn't using all of it - just 12MP, but we'll elaborate on that later on. The point is - great main camera chops. The other cameras aren't all that special but complete a capable tri-set. There's an autofocusing ultrawide that's at the reasonable end of the 'ultrawide' spectrum instead of the extreme. As for zoom, gone is the dual focal length periscope of the Xperia 1 III, replaced on the Pro-I with a more modest 2x-only unit.
